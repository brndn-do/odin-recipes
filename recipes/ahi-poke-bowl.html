Ahi Poke Bowl

Description

Ahi poke bowl is a refreshing Hawaiian dish featuring chunks of fresh, sushi-grade Ahi tuna marinated in a savory blend of soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. This flavorful base is paired with vibrant ingredients like creamy avocado, crisp cucumber slices, and delicate radishes, all served atop a bed of fluffy sushi rice or crisp greens. Garnished with sesame seeds and scallions, the bowl is elevated with a drizzle of spicy mayo or sriracha, adding a kick of heat. Each bite offers a harmonious mix of savory tuna and refreshing vegetables, creating a satisfying and flavorful meal reminiscent of the tropical shores of Hawaii.

Ingredients

  • Sushi-grade Ahi tuna, cubed
  • Soy Sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Rice Vinegar
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Cucumber, thinly sliced
  • Radishes, thinly sliced
  • Cooked sushi rice
  • Sesame seeds
  • Scallions, thinly sliced
  • Spicy mayo

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Begin by cubing the sushi-grade Ahi tuna. Slice the avocado, cucumber, and radishes thinly. Also, thinly slice the scallions.
  2. Marinate the Tuna: In a mixing bowl, combine the cubed Ahi tuna with soy sauce (to taste), sesame oil (about 1 tablespoon), and rice vinegar (about 1 tablespoon). Toss gently to coat the tuna evenly. Set aside to marinate while you prepare other ingredients.
  3. Prepare the Base: If using cooked sushi rice, divide it evenly among serving bowls. Alternatively, arrange mixed greens as the base in each bowl.
  4. Assemble the Poke Bowls: Divide the marinated tuna evenly among the serving bowls over the rice or greens.
  5. Add the Vegetables: Arrange the sliced avocado, cucumber, and radishes around the tuna in each bowl.
  6. Garnish with Sesame Seeds and Scallions: Sprinkle sesame seeds and thinly sliced scallions over each bowl for added texture and flavor.
  7. Drizzle with Sauce: Drizzle each bowl with spicy mayo or sriracha sauce according to taste preferences.
